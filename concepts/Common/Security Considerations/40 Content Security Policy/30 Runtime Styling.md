All DevExtreme libraries (except [DevExtreme Map]({currentpath}/#Content_Security_Policy/CSP_Directives_for_Map_Integrations)) use CSSOM assignments to update styles at runtime (**element**.**style**.[\_cssProperty\_](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/CSS/Reference) and **element**.**style**.[cssText](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/API/CSSStyleDeclaration/cssText)). For instance, our internal DOM adapter defines a custom [setAttribute](https://github.com/DevExpress/DevExtreme/blob/26_1/packages/devextreme/js/__internal/core/m_dom_adapter.ts#L140-L146) function that calls **element**.**style**.**cssText** to update styles. DevExtreme components also use CSSOM APIs ([adoptedStyleSheets](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/API/ShadowRoot/adoptedStyleSheets) and [insertRule](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/API/CSSStyleSheet/insertRule)) to propagate styles in a [Shadow DOM](/Documentation/Guide/Common/Shadow_DOM/).

DevExtreme libraries do not inject `<style>` elements at runtime. This allows DevExtreme-powered apps to support `style-src 'self'` CSP rules without `'unsafe-inline'`.

We validate our [JS technical demos](https://js.devexpress.com/Demos/WidgetsGallery/) (jQuery, Angular, Vue, and React) against `style-src 'self'` without `'unsafe-inline'` in our development workflows (CI). However, this practice does not guarantee that a future release will not introduce runtime styling incompatible with this rule. To ensure your application's continuous compliance with `style-src 'self'` without `'unsafe-inline'`, configure a report-only [securitypolicyviolation](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/API/Document/securitypolicyviolation_event) listener as a regression check in your CI systems.
